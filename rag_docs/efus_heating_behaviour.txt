KEY FINDINGS ON HEATING BEHAVIOUR:

The average UK household heats their home to 18 degrees Celsius in the living room.
Reducing thermostat by 1 degree saves approximately £145 per year on heating bills.
Reducing thermostat by 2 degrees saves approximately £290 per year on heating bills.

Heating patterns:
- 40 percent of households heat their home all day when someone is at home
- 35 percent use a timer to heat only morning and evening
- 25 percent heat only when they feel cold

Households that use a timer or programmer save on average £75 per year compared to heating all day.
Households that use a smart thermostat save on average £120 per year.

Hot water heating accounts for approximately 16 percent of energy bills.
Reducing shower time by 1 minute per day saves approximately £7 per year per person.
Replacing a bath with a shower saves approximately £20 per year.
Fixing a dripping hot tap saves approximately £18 per year.

Draught proofing:
- Draught proofing windows and doors saves approximately £45 per year.
- Fitting a draught excluder to a letterbox saves approximately £15 per year.

Appliance behaviour:
- Turning off appliances from standby saves approximately £35 per year.
- Using a washing machine at 30 degrees instead of 40 saves approximately £28 per year.
- Running a full dishwasher instead of half loads saves approximately £14 per year.
- Switching to LED lighting saves approximately £55 per year.

London specific:
- London households spend on average 8 percent more on energy than the UK average due to higher property prices and older housing stock.
- 58 percent of London households live in flats compared to 20 percent nationally.
- London flats typically have lower heating costs but higher electricity costs than houses.

[Section 1: Space Heating and Thermostat Behaviour]
The Energy Follow-Up Survey (EFUS) monitors how UK residents actually interact with their heating systems, providing highly actionable insights for behavioral adjustments:

1. Thermostat Settings:
- The average winter thermostat setting in UK living rooms is 20°C.
- Turning the thermostat down by just 1°C (from 20°C to 19°C) reduces space heating gas consumption by approximately 10%. For a typical mid-terrace house, this equates to an average annual saving of £80 to £120.

2. Heating Season Patterns:
- The typical winter heating pattern is a "twice-a-day" timer schedule: 2 hours of heat in the morning and 5 to 7 hours of heat in the evening.
- Over 70% of households keep their heating on even when rooms are unoccupied. Switching to "on-demand" heating or programming zone-specific Thermostatic Radiator Valves (TRVs) saves up to 15% on heating distribution losses.

[Section 2: Domestic Hot Water Habits]
Water heating is the second largest consumer of household energy, and EFUS tracks how water use directly impacts utility bills:

1. Shower vs. Bath Consumption:
- Showers account for the largest single share of household hot water consumption.
- A standard showerhead uses 12 to 15 liters of water per minute. Replacing it with an eco-aerated showerhead drops consumption to 8 liters per minute without sacrificing water pressure. This simple swap reduces gas water-heating costs and water volumetric charges (for metered bills) by 30%.
- Restricting shower times to 4 minutes per person saves an average household up to £45 in water bills and £50 in gas heating costs annually.
