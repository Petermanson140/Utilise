  <h1 class="gem-c-heading__text govuk-heading-xl">
    Help from your energy supplier: the Energy Company Obligation
</h1></div>
  </div>
</div>
<div class="govuk-grid-row">
  <div class="govuk-grid-column-two-thirds">
    <div class="responsive-bottom-margin">
      <div data-module="govspeak" class="gem-c-govspeak govuk-govspeak gem-c-govspeak--direction-ltr js-disable-youtube govuk-!-margin-bottom-0">
    
      
<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
  <p>There are quality issues with some solid wall insulation fitted through the Energy Company Obligation scheme. <a href="/guidance/what-to-do-if-you-have-poor-quality-wall-insulation">Check what to do if you’re concerned about the insulation you’ve had fitted through a government scheme</a>.</p>
</div>

<p>You might be able to get help for energy-saving improvements to your home if you either:</p>

<ul>
  <li>claim certain benefits and live in private housing (for example you own your home or rent from a private landlord)</li>
  <li>live in social housing</li>
</ul>

<p>You may get help with the cost of:</p>

<ul>
  <li>insulation work, for example to your loft or cavity walls</li>
  <li>replacing or repairing your boiler - or other upgrades to your heating</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="if-you-claim-benefits-and-live-in-private-housing">If you claim benefits and live in private housing</h2>

<p>You might be eligible for help if you live in private housing and get one of the following benefits:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Universal Credit</li>
  <li>Pension Guarantee Credit</li>
  <li>Pension Savings Credit</li>
  <li>Income Support</li>
  <li>income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (<abbr title="Jobseeker’s Allowance">JSA</abbr>)</li>
  <li>income-related Employment and Support Allowance (<abbr title="Employment and Support Allowance">ESA</abbr>)</li>
  <li>Child Benefit</li>
  <li>Housing Benefit</li>
</ul>

<p>If you own your house, it must have an energy efficiency rating of D, E, F or G to be eligible.</p>

<p>If you rent from a private landlord, the house must have an energy efficiency rating of E, F or G to be eligible. You must have the owner’s permission to do the work.</p>

<p>Use the <a href="/find-energy-certificate">energy performance certificate register</a> to find your property’s energy efficiency rating, or ask your landlord.</p>

<h2 id="if-youre-a-social-housing-tenant">If you’re a social housing tenant</h2>

<p>If you live in social housing that has an energy efficiency rating of E, F or G you might be eligible for help with insulation or installing a heating system for the first time.</p>

<p>Use the <a href="/find-energy-certificate">energy performance certificate register</a> to find your property’s energy efficiency rating, or ask your landlord or housing association.</p>

<h2 id="apply-for-help">Apply for help</h2>

<p>Contact <a href="/find-local-council">your local council</a> to find out if they’re taking part in the Energy Company Obligation scheme, or contact an energy supplier directly.</p>

<p>You can find contact details of suppliers who are taking part in the scheme on the <a rel="external" href="https://www.ofgem.gov.uk/environmental-and-social-schemes/energy-company-obligation-eco/energy-company-obligation-eco-contacts-guidance-and-resources/eco-supplier-contact-details">Ofgem website</a>.</p>

<div role="note" aria-label="Information" class="application-notice info-notice">
  <p>Installers or agents might contact you directly. If you’re not sure if an offer is genuine, contact <a href="/find-local-council">your local council</a> or the energy supplier they represent to check.</p>
</div>

<p>Get more information and advice on <a href="/improve-energy-efficiency">ways to save energy in your home</a>.</p>

<h2 id="if-youre-unhappy-with-any-work">If you’re unhappy with any work</h2>

<p><a href="/guidance/what-to-do-if-you-have-poor-quality-wall-insulation">Check what to do if you’re concerned about solid wall insulation you’ve had fitted through the Energy Company Obligation scheme</a>.</p>

<p>If you’re unhappy with other work or products installed under the scheme, contact either the installer or the company that guaranteed the work.</p>

<p>If they do not solve the problem, <a rel="external" href="https://www.citizensadvice.org.uk/consumer/protection-for-the-consumer/citizens-advice-consumer-service/if-you-need-more-help-about-a-consumer-issue/">contact Citizens Advice</a>.</p>
