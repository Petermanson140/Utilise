[Section 2: Real-world Savings from Energy Efficiency Measures]
NEED tracks the actual median gas savings achieved in the year immediately following the installation of specific energy efficiency interventions:

1. Cavity Wall Insulation:
- Median gas saving: 8% to 10%
- Average annual bill reduction: £100 - £150 (based on typical energy pricing)
- Property applicability: Most homes built between 1920 and 1990.

2. Loft Insulation (Professional or DIY installation):
- Median gas saving: 4% to 5% (higher if upgrading from completely uninsulated lofts)
- Average annual bill reduction: £50 - £80
- Property applicability: Any property with an accessible roof space.

3. Solid Wall Insulation (External or Internal):
- Median gas saving: 15% to 22%
- Average annual bill reduction: £250 - £350
- Property applicability: Pre-1920 solid-brick properties (extremely common in London).

4. Condensing Boiler Upgrade:
- Median gas saving: 9% to 12%
- Property applicability: Replacing older, non-condensing G-rated gas boilers.

WHY NATIONAL AVERAGES ARE MISLEADING:
UK-wide median domestic consumption (10,000 kWh gas / 2,500 kWh electricity) is skewed up by detached and semi-detached properties predominant in other regions. In Greater London, smaller floor areas and high flat density lead to lower space heating needs but higher baseline electricity usage per sq meter.

LONDON-SPECIFIC EFFICIENCY INSIGHTS:
- 73% of new-build London properties use communal heating / district heat networks rather than individual gas boilers.
- Solid wall insulation in pre-1919 London converted flats reduces space heating demand by an average of 18-22%.
- Glazing upgrades (single to double/triple) in Victorian/Edwardian converted flats yield an average 12% gas reduction.
